Tests of statistical features of Fourier transformations

Summary

This page shows how expected values and variances changes in Fourier transformations. This is useful in determining how SNR and SINAD changes. SNR is usually improved at the expense of specificity of location. SNR is dependent on the sampling set. The relation is usually that SNR improves with the square root of the sampling set size. Somewhat lower than expected SNR exists for n-radix intervals for lower n due to zero elements in the basis vectors.

Derivation

The result of straightforward arithmetics on expected value and variance.
